	<h2>Research</h2>
	<p>The Stieber Lab focuses on solving problems through complementary efforts in synthetic inorganic chemistry, spectroscopy and computational chemistry. Current directions in the group include:</p>
    <p></p>
    <p>&#x25cf; Expanding the scope of X-ray emission spectroscopy to allow for identification of small molecules (eg. NO, NO<sup>1-</sup>, NO<sup>2-</sup>) bound to transition metal centers. Results of this work will be applied to understanding how biological systems reduce and capture airborne pollutants.
    <br>
    <br>&#x25cf; Synthesize novel first-row transition metal compounds for benchmarking spectroscopic signatures of small molecules bound to metal centers.
    <br>
    <br>&#x25cf; Development of environmentally friendly first row transition metal catalysts for transformations such as C-C bond formation and polymerizations.
    <br>
    <br>&#x25cf; Multiplet (Configuration Interaction) calculations of f-block systems and spectra to understand bonding with f-elements.
    <br>
    <br>&#x25cf; Synthesis of lanthanide complexes with redox-active ligands.
    </p>
